Cooking with intention begins long before the flame: in the choice of produce.
When an ingredient is in season and treated with respect, the dish needs less
artifice and says more. That idea holds together every Alma Mía proposal.

## Produce first

A seasonal menu is not a limitation, it is a compass. Fruit at its peak, the
vegetables of the moment and the catch of the day set the path and allow a
wholesome Mediterranean cuisine that feels light and complete at once.

## Intention in every step

To intend a dish is to decide why it is there: what texture it brings, how it
speaks to the rest of the table and what feeling it leaves. That care is what
turns a meal into an experience.

## A table that accompanies

Styling — the light, the dried flowers, the lavender, the linen — is not empty
decoration: it prepares the senses. Eating well also means feeling well
accompanied.
